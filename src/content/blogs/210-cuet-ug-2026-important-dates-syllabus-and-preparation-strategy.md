<h2>CUET UG 2026: Your Gateway to Top Central Universities</h2>
<p>The <strong>CUET UG 2026</strong> has become the most important exam for students seeking admission into Central Universities like DU, JNU, and BHU. With over 1.5 million applicants, the competition is at an all-time high.</p>

<h3>Exam Dates and Schedule</h3>
<p>The National Testing Agency (NTA) has scheduled the CUET UG exams to begin in the third week of May 2026. Admit cards are expected to be released by the first week of May.</p>

<h3>Preparation Tips for 100th Percentile</h3>
<ol>
  <li><strong>Focus on NCERT:</strong> The majority of the domain-specific questions are derived directly from NCERT textbooks.</li>
  <li><strong>General Test Mastery:</strong> Don't ignore the logical reasoning and current affairs section.</li>
  <li><strong>Mock Tests:</strong> Practice with NTA's official mock tests to get used to the Computer Based Test (CBT) environment.</li>
</ol>

<h3>Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)</h3>
<div class="faq-section">
  <div class="faq-item">
    <h4>What is the marking scheme for CUET UG 2026?</h4>
    <p>The exam follows a +5 for correct answers and -1 for incorrect answers marking scheme.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="faq-item">
    <h4>Can I change my subjects after registration?</h4>
    <p>No, the correction window has closed. Candidates must appear for the subjects they selected during the application process.</p>
