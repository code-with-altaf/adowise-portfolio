<h2>From 'What is AI?' to 'How do I use my AI Agent?'</h2>
<p>In 2024, the world was asking what AI was. By 2026, the question has changed completely. AI is no longer a 'topic'—it is a 'utility,' much like electricity or the internet itself. Queries like "AI agents" and "automated workflows" now trend daily, reflecting how the technology has integrated into our phones, cars, and workplaces.</p>

<h3>The Democratization of Intelligence</h3>
<p>The most evergreen trend in AI is the search for 'How-To' applications. People aren't just curious about the math; they want to know how to automate their emails, generate images for their business, or summarize complex legal documents. At Adowise, we've seen a 300% increase in requests for custom AI agents tailored to these specific 'utility' tasks.</p>

<h3>AI and Economic Stress</h3>
<p>One interesting intersection we see in 2026 is between AI and inflation. Searchers are increasingly looking for AI tools that help them compare prices, find second-hand luxury deals, or manage their household budgets. AI has become a weapon against economic stress, allowing users to make smarter, more data-driven decisions in real-time.</p>

<h3>The Ethical Debate is Evergreen</h3>
<p>While the tech changes, the human concerns remain the same. Debates over "AI ethics" and "job displacement" trend every single day. This is a healthy sign of a society adapting to a massive technological shift. We believe that 'Brand Trust' in the AI era will be built on transparency—businesses that disclose how they use AI will win the long-term loyalty of their customers.</p>
